## Demo script

This is another "Bobcat" experiment.
It's actually the third, but I'll come back to the second one.
I should have called this one Lil' Bobüçî, but Lil' Doc is fine and not too on the nose.
The original idea here is to reduce my document editor ideas down to something really simplistic.
(I have a bigger idea brewing, but wanted to shore up my collaborating-with-agents workflow first.)
This is about $6 of tokens in Amp. 
What I've learned:
* Amp, it's pretty good. Like it says on the tin, an opinionated stew of good ideas about how a coding agent should work.
* Collaborating with an agent on building a single-platform (macOS) application is much easier than trying to target multiple platforms via Catalyst.
* There are lots of fiddly bits to get right once you start trying to daily a document editor like this.
* But, you can't really know whether progress or something good is being made unless you can find a reason to daily drive your work. Eventually, I'll need to find an excuse to use this.

## Feb 5, 2026

I kind of got bored about this one pretty quickly. 
First, because I quickly fell off a cliff where I could not easily prompt Amp to change the UI in the way I want
And, amp was often making mistakes with introducing bugs in how the system worked or was unable to find those bugs. 
Anyway, the amp was a great zero-to-one pair, but not a one-to-two collaborator. 

Second, I think an editor built for moving around in notation like Canon Cat is interesting but of limited utility to my actual uses. 
In reality I'm always going to be using a Bear or Things or Day One or Ulysses. 
some kind of pretty dang good markdown editor
I'm not going to try and replace or supllant those. 

So I think the next step is to pursue developing a notation system of my own within those other ecosystems. 
For example in veins or in bare patch symbols that I can search through and find quickly. 
And maybe encode that notation and navigate through it with system automation, like short cuts, or Apple scripts, or Raycast, or something like that. 
So you know kind of building low road software in this next prototype kind of feels like the next step. 

That said, I would like to return to the idea of a notation, a rule-based notation editor just to build that for fun, 
which is kind of what I had in mind to build in the first place when I built Lil Doc
it would be a useful experiment to see if I could get past that quality cliff and produce what I think is a small scope but high quality macOS application.
